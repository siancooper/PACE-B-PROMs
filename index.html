PACE-B Trial: Patient-Reported Outcomes

What do the quality-of-life results mean for patients choosing between stereotactic radiotherapy (SBRT) and conventional radiotherapy for prostate cancer?

The PACE-B trial compared two types of radiotherapy for localised prostate cancer: SBRT (5 treatments over 1–2 weeks) versus conventional radiotherapy (20 or 39 treatments over 4–8 weeks). These charts show what happened to patients’ quality of life — specifically their urinary, bowel and sexual function — at 2 years after treatment.

Each square in the waffle chart represents 1 in 100 patients. Coloured squares show how many patients reported that symptom.
